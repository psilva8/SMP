About Hair Tattoo Procedures

Scalp Micropigmentation (SMP) is a non-surgical, advanced cosmetic tattoo technique that replicates the appearance of hair follicles on the scalp. This innovative procedure creates the illusion of a closely shaved head for those with hair loss or thinning hair.

SMP uses specialized equipment and pigments to deposit tiny dots on the scalp, matching your natural hair color and creating a natural-looking hairline. Unlike traditional hair transplants, SMP requires minimal downtime and provides immediate results.

The procedure is suitable for various types of hair loss, including male pattern baldness, alopecia, thinning hair, and scalp scars. SMP can also be used to enhance the results of a hair transplant by adding density to the appearance of the transplanted area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most SMP procedures require 2-4 sessions, each lasting 2-4 hours depending on the extent of the treatment area. These sessions are typically spaced 10-14 days apart.

Most clients report minimal discomfort during the procedure. Topical numbing cream is typically applied before the treatment to minimize any pain.

Scalp micropigmentation can last 4-6 years on average before a touch-up is needed. The longevity depends on factors like skin type, lifestyle, and sun exposure.

There's minimal downtime with SMP. The scalp may appear red for 1-2 days after each session, but most clients can return to normal activities immediately.
